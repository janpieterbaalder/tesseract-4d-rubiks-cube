    How to play

    A 4D Rubik's cube has 8 cubic cells instead of 6 flat faces. It's drawn as a small cube nested inside a larger frame — the six “tunnels” between them are six more cells, and one cell stays hidden at the very front. Goal: make every cell a single colour.

    Twist & solve
    • Tap/Click a sticker — selects its cell (it lights up) and opens the twist panel. Press the ↺ / ↻ buttons to turn it.
    • Select an edge or corner block and the panel adds its diagonal grip: a 180° flip or 120° spin.
    • Keyboard: 1 2 3 twist the selected cell's planes · hold ⇧ to reverse.
    Bring any cell to the centre
    • Ctrl+click a cell (desktop) — or hold a cell (touch) — and it spins to the middle. This is how you reveal the hidden cell. It never changes the puzzle.
    Look around
    • Drag orbit in 3D · scroll/pinch zoom — zooming follows your cursor, not the centre.
    • Pan freely: right-drag or Alt+drag (desktop), two-finger drag (touch).
    • Rotate through the 4th dimension: ⇧+drag.
    • Lost? View in the bottom bar or V puts everything back.
    Game
    • Scramble · Undo · Reset in the bottom bar · keys S U R
