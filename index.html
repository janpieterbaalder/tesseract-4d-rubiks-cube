How to play

A 4D Rubik's cube has 8 cubic cells instead of 6 flat faces. It's drawn as a small cube nested inside a large one — the six “tunnels” between them are the other six cells. Make every cell a single colour.

  • Drag Orbit the view in 3D
  • Scroll Zoom
  • Click a tile to select its cell
  • Twist panel Turn the selected cell (or keys 123, hold ⇧ to reverse)
  • 4D rotation Bring hidden cells into view
  • S Scramble · U Undo · R Reset
